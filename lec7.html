[Lecture 7] Conditionals - incorportating Boolean logic into programs

Imagine you are programming a selfdriving car. The car has to take a sequence of decisions as it finds its way to the destination. Ideally, we want these decisions to be absolutely logical lest the car gets caught in an accident or traffic violation. For example: If the signal light glows red, it has to stop at a signal; else if it glows orange, we want it to proceed with caution, else if it is green we proceed without any worries. Suppose, the decision of when to apply a brake will be logical only when you take various parameters into account. These parameters may include speed, distance from the obstacle, traffic, size of the vehicale etc - into account. If you mindlessly hit the break, that may lead some serious disaster and you'll end cursing yourself you have acted mindlessly! A decision is said to be logical when it is taken subject to certain conditons.
